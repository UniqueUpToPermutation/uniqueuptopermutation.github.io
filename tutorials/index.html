Tutorials

In my free time, I like to create tutorials about mathematics and computer graphics. I'm particularly interested in talking about the link between stochastic processes (e.g., Lagrangian viewpoint) and partial differential equations (e.g., Eulerian viewpoint).  

Partial Differential Equations from Stochastic Processes

These tutorials are about how you can derive partial differential equations from the expectation of functional of a stochastic process. It covers both discrete processes (on graphs) in part I and continuous processes (stochastic differential equations) in part II.

Rendering Theory

A series of tutorials about the mathematical underpinnings of offline and real-time rendering in computer graphics. It covers radiative transport, as well as how to simulate radiative transport with Monte Carlo algorithms, and some common techniques and models used computer graphics.

Differential Equations from Stochastic Processes: Part II

A tutorial on the deep connection between elliptic/parabolic systems and stochastic processes. The connection gives a lot of useful intuition about how to interpret elliptic/parabolic PDEs as well as Laplacian systems on graphs. Part II covers continuous time stochastic processes.
